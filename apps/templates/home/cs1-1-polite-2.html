When interacting with an anxious patient, apart from using compassionate and listening facial expressions we covered in the Expression Practice section, we suggest to try the following:
1) Speak clearly, slowly, and at an appropriate volume
2) Appear engaged, pleasant and calm
3) Maintain appropriate eye contact
4) Sit/stand at eye level with the patient
5) Avoid sudden movements
6) Pay attention to patient cues (i.e. tensing muscles, fidgeting, breathing quickly, flushing, crying, trembling, appearing distracted or spaced out)
