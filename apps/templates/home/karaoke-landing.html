Expression Practice

We saw that recognizing facial expressions might be hard! Now let's try to practice some of them. This tool is meant for expression practice so that you can reflect and see what works best for you. These are non-verbal guidelines, backed by scientific research, that are intended to build report with a patient.
We’re not trying to be formulaic. We don’t know the context that you’ll have with a patient, and we know that matters greatly.


Compassionate expression
A compassionate facial expression can make a patient feel understood, supported and valued.

Welcoming expression
A welcoming facial expression can help establish rapport, trust and comfort with the patient.

Neutral expression
A neutral facial expression can help the doctor maintain objectivity, professionalism and respect for the patient.
