Expression Practice

Clinicians tend to underestimate their own cognitive and affective states during these difficult patient presentations. Many clinicians identify with valorized medical ideals, such as saving lives and healing, which can be complicated by repeated difficult presentations of these patients. OUD, depending on its acuity, might challenge these ideals and a clinician's sense of feeling helpful to their patients. For the previous reasons discussed, senior clinicians often are the most jaded. After a career of such difficulties, their opinions about individuals with OUD are not just negative, they are solidified. It is indubitable that these opinions and perspectives of senior clinicians impact younger clinicians' views. This is called the “hidden curriculum” of medical training: the unspoken attitudes and small details that supervising physicians convey when engaging with patients.

Let's practice controlling your facial expressions when you interact with your patients! We've found that people commonly have less control and understanding of their expression than they think. So, we've designed Expression Practice, a tool to help you to improve that. Choose an expression to practice below!
